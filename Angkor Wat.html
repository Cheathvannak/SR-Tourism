Angkor Wat

Geography and Founder

This temple is located in Northwest Cambodia, it is the largest religious stucture in the world by land area. It was built in the 12th century by King Suryavarman II.

Summarized History

Angkor was abandoned within the early 15th century. Angkor Wat, which remained a vital pilgrimage site and continued to draw in European visitors, was still maintained by Theravada Buddhist monks. After the establishment of the French colonial regime in 1863, Angkor Wat was "rediscovered."
